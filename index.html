Tatiana is a Russian-born photographer based in Toronto. She graduated from Ryerson University with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Photography.

Her artwork explores social trends – often through landscape photography. She is interested in human relationship to nature. Her work examines the way people inhabit land and the way they alter it. The North-American phenomenon of suburbia is one of the subject matters Tatiana has explored; a theme particularly personal, because she spent her teenage years among the orderly rows of houses. Farming – another subject matter – is one of the oldest and most basic ways people relate to and depend upon nature. Tatiana finds the landscape and gesture of farming wholesome and beautiful.

In her photographic work, Tatiana hopes to explore larger issues that impact nature and society through local manifestations. Her aim is to create images that foster thought and questions. Equally important for her is to create works of esthetic interest, offering the viewer a space of beautiful light, shape and colour to contemplate the underlying topics.
